WHAT IS A HEALTHY, RATIONAL, BALANCED NUTRITION?
It has long been known that Eastern medicine considers almost all diseases to be the result of improper (or poor-quality) nutrition, which disables internal organs. Recent studies by modern scientists are increasingly confirming this theory of the occurrence of many diseases. We know that in addition to calories with food, we get the vital energy we need, but it and calories are not identical quantities. How often well-fed people look sluggish and apathetic. The stomach is stuffed "to the eyeballs", and the vitality is at zero. Many people who switch to a healthy diet later notice that from the right food and the thoughts in their heads are correct: harmonious, non-aggressive, constructive, creative. Even simple observance of moderation in nutrition can work wonders for healing ... That is, food can also be healthy!

Healthy nutrition (healthy diet) is nutrition that ensures the growth, normal development and vital activity of a person, contributes to strengthening his health and preventing diseases. A healthy diet combined with regular exercise reduces the risk of chronic diseases and disorders such as obesity, cardiovascular disease, diabetes, high blood pressure and cancer. In medicine, diseases caused by an unhealthy diet are called alimentary (Latin alimentarius - related to nutrition) - i.e. diseases caused by insufficient or excessive intake of nutrients in comparison with physiological needs. These also include iron deficiency anemia, various iodine deficiency diseases, protein-calorie deficiency, etc.
Food hygiene is a branch of hygiene that studies the problems of wholesome food and rational nutrition of a healthy person. In the process of development, dietology emerged from food hygiene, studying the nutrition of patients and developing the principles of therapeutic nutrition. An independent section is the study of nutrition at an early age.

Nutritional hygiene (hereinafter referred to as GP) - studies human nutrition depending on gender, age, profession, nature of work, physical activity, climatic conditions, national and other characteristics, the quantitative and qualitative side of the nutrition of the population of various regions and determines the need for people in nutrients resp. living and working conditions.

food in the school canteen The practical tasks of the GP include the development of rational nutrition in labor collectives (at factories, state farms, collective farms, etc., children in schools, preschool and other institutions); development of preventive nutrition for workers employed in enterprises with occupational hazards; development of methods for effective sanitary control at public catering enterprises and the food industry - to protect food from the possible penetration of harmful substances into them; development of measures for the prevention of food poisoning, toxic infections and intoxications, preventive sanitary supervision of the design, construction and commissioning of food industry, trade and public catering enterprises, participation in the development of GOSTs and temporary specifications (VTU) for new food products.
PRINCIPLES OF RATIONAL NUTRITION
Nutrition is at the center of medical attention today. In all countries, interest in them is constantly growing among the most diverse segments of the population, scientists and government agencies.

The rapid increase in the world's population requires a corresponding increase in the production of food resources and foodstuffs - this is one of the main problems that determine the progress of the earth's civilization.
Recently, a huge number of scientifically unproven dietary recommendations for a wide range of the population have appeared in the press, which can cause irreparable harm to health. In connection with the relevance of issues related to nutrition, it is worth familiarizing yourself with the principles of rational nutrition, based on the many years of work of Academician A. A. Pokrovsky, and which are still guided by specialists from Rospotrebnadzor and the Ministry of Health.
BASIC POWER FUNCTIONS

1. Supplying the body with energy.

In this sense, a person can be compared to any machine that does work, but requires fuel for this. Rational nutrition provides an approximate balance of energy entering the body, expended on ensuring vital processes.

2. Supply of the body with plastic substances

Plastic substances primarily include proteins, to a lesser extent, minerals, fats, and, to an even lesser extent, carbohydrates. In the process of vital activity in the human body, some cells and intracellular structures are constantly destroyed and others appear in their place. The building materials for creating new cells and intracellular structures are the chemicals that make up food products. The need for plastic food substances varies depending on age: in children, such a need is increased (after all, they are used not only to replace destroyed cells and intracellular structures, but also to carry out growth processes), and in older people it is reduced.

3. Supply of the body with biologically active substances

Biologically active substances are necessary for the regulation of vital processes. Enzymes and most hormones - regulators of chemical processes occurring in the body - are synthesized by the body itself. However, some coenzymes (an essential component of enzymes), without which enzymes cannot exercise their activity, as well as some hormones, the human body can synthesize only from special precursors found in food. These precursors are vitamins present in foods.

4. Development of immunity, both non-specific and specific

It was found that the magnitude of the immune response to infection depends on the quality of nutrition and, especially, on the sufficient content of calories, complete proteins and vitamins in food. With insufficient nutrition, general immunity decreases and the body's resistance to various infections decreases. Conversely, a nutritious diet with sufficient protein, fat, vitamins and calories strengthens the immune system and increases resistance to infections. In this case, we are talking about the relationship of nutrition with nonspecific immunity. Later it was found that a certain part of the chemical compounds contained in food is not broken down in the digestive tract or is only partially broken down. Such large non-split molecules of proteins or polypeptides can penetrate the intestinal wall into the blood and, being foreign to the body, cause its specific immune response.

Studies conducted at the Institute of Nutrition of the Russian Academy of Medical Sciences found that a few percent (or a few tenths of a percent) of proteins that come with food are found in the blood, liver and some other internal organs in the form of large molecules that retain the antigenic properties of the original food proteins. It was also revealed that specific antibodies are produced in the body against these foreign food proteins. Thus, in the process of nutrition, there is a constant flow of antigens from the digestive tract into the internal environment of the body, which leads to the development and maintenance of specific immunity to food proteins.
BALANCED NUTRITION
A balanced diet means the nutritional value of the foods we eat. There are two meanings here: First, enough calories and nutrients to avoid malnutrition; The second is the physiological balance of various nutrients in order to promote health and prevent chronic diseases (for example, the proportionality between the three important energy carriers: sugars, fats, proteins).

How many chemicals the body of an adult consumes in the process of life, the same amount should come with food. However, in the process of metabolism, some substances can pass into others. At the same time, most of them can be synthesized in the body, while some are, as it were, initial: they cannot be synthesized and must be supplied with food. Hence, all nutrients are divided into interchangeable and irreplaceable. The latter include essential amino acids (valine, isoleucine, leucine, lysine, methionine, threonine, tryptophan and phenylalanine), essential fatty acids (linoleic, linolenic), vitamins and minerals. Those. in the food consumed should be: a balance between the necessary amino acids (within the protein); balance between saturated and unsaturated fatty acids; balance between carbohydrates and dietary fiber; balance between acid-forming and alkaline-forming food; balance between animal and vegetable products, etc.
